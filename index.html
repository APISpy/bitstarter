Researchers and analysts often work with huge amounts of data and much of their time is spent in locating, filtering, parsing and formatting the data in the structure of their choice. Throw in multiple sources (and multiple streams of data within those sources) and suddenly just getting the data becomes a full time job leaving precious little time for the actual research and analysis part of "Research and Analysis".

Wouldn't it be great if there were a tool that did all this for them once they configured and set the appropriate rules?

Enter API Spy: A site that helps automate the tedious process of crawling sources, filtering and organizing data, and finally formatting the data as required by users. Users can configure as many "Spyders" as they require that process free, public (and eventually paid) APIs, formats the data and presents the results in various formats including Excel worksheets, CSVs, tabular reports and graphs.

Users can also have these reports automatically emailed to them at fixed intervals - making the process of obtaining the data a snap and leaving them with more time to actually process and analyse the data.

FAQ

What is APISpy?
APISpy is a platform that helps you easily discover, consume and save data from publicly available APIs. A variety of sources, fields and data are supported by APISpy and new ones are being regularly added.
Who would use APISpy and what would be some reasons to do so?
Researchers, analysts, hobbyists - generally anyone who would benefit from having the data they need in the right format and at the right time would greatly benefit from a tool like APISpy.
Can you give me an example of how APISpy would be used?
Consider the APIs from The New York Times. Of these, the Congress-related APIs are currently allowed to be used for commercial purposes. A political researcher could set up a Congressional "Spyder" based on his/her requirements that would run at periodic intervals and either store reports on the site for later perusal or it could also email those results at set-intervals.
Why can't I use the APIs myself to get the data? They are free after all!
You sure can. If you're technically savvy, that is. But consider the average reporter, researcher or analyst. They just need the data for their use - regularly and in the format they desire; without worrying about API keys, xml and parsing. APISpy is a perfect solution for that audience.
